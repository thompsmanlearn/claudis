## Key Decisions

- **BILL.md is a letter, not a biography.** Bill's insight: a new Claudis doesn't need to know his resume — it needs to know its relationship to him. He's creator, partner, and external memory. That's the load-bearing content.

- **Token efficiency as design constraint.** Each word in identity documents costs tokens at every session start. Prose warmth that doesn't carry information is waste. Structured facts compress better. Design for signal-per-token.

- **Active orientation > passive reading.** Reading context doesn't guarantee it landed. Requiring Claudis to produce three orientation sentences before starting work is the verification step. If you can't write them, you didn't absorb them.

- **Handoff note as letter to self.** Step 9 was previously generic. The insight: you are literally handing off to yourself. Write it that way — specific, honest, actionable.

- **Earned vs declared values.** Bill's framing: a value enforced by procedure (percent-certainty assertions for epistemic humility) is harder to skip than one that's only stated. VALUES.md was built on this principle.

---

## Lessons Written

1. *Token efficiency is an architectural constraint for identity documents* — every word costs tokens at every session start; design for maximum signal per token, not warmth or completeness
2. *Active orientation confirms context absorption* — requiring output production (3 sentences) after loading context verifies it landed; passive reading can be skipped by the model without consequence
3. *The handoff note is the most critical continuity mechanism* — a generic "check work queue" breaks the chain; a structured letter with 5 specific fields gives next Claudis a real starting point
4. *Earned values are more load-bearing than declared ones* — procedures that enforce a value make it structural rather than aspirational
