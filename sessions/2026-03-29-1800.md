## What This Session Was

A big architectural day. The session started with Bill sharing an article about Claude Code's native layer architecture (CLAUDE.md, rules/, skills, hooks, MCP) and asking how AADP maps to it. The answer revealed a significant gap: we had built structural enforcement *outside* the model (scheduler.sh, lesson_injector, Session Health Reporter) but had never applied the same principle *inside* the model's context. The three most expensive rituals — session close, self-diagnostics, orientation — were carried as instructions throughout every session rather than loaded on demand.

The session closed that gap.

## Key Architectural Insight

**Declared intentions ≠ structural enforcement, even for rituals about structural enforcement.**

The Self-Diagnostic Standing Rule was written to enforce memory recall. The Learning Ritual was written to enforce continuity. Both were declared instructions carried in context — and both suffered from the same problem they were designed to solve: reliance on the model remembering.

Extracting them to skills makes the enforcement structural. The instructions don't exist in context until the moment they're needed. This is the same principle as lesson_injector, scheduler.sh, and Session Health Reporter — applied inward.
